    Seeing Japan

    • by ANUchannel
    • 1 month ago
    • 343 views
    The land of the cherry blossom is brightening the ANU campus, with an exhibition of artwork inspired by Japan - 'Seeing Japan' - currently on display.

    In this video we talk to exhibition organiser Jill Mowbray-Tsutsumi and artist Michael Le Grand about what inspires them about Japan, as well as showcasing some of the eye-popping items and talent on display.

    'Seeing Japan' is an exhibition exploring the artists' interaction and engagement with the diversity of the Japanese experience. The exhibition showcases a collection of works including sculpture, ceramics, textiles, photography and jewellery by ANU students, staff and alumni who have been influenced by Japan in their lives and works.

    'Seeing Japan' forms part of the Japan Studies Association of Australia 2013 conference. The exhibition is hosted by the ANU School of Art and organised by the Japan Institute in the ANU College of Asia and the Pacific.

    Engaging Visions - Artists and rural Aussies engage on the environment

    • by ANUchannel
    • 3 years ago
    • 1,063 views
    John Reid, Rod Lamberts and Carolyn Young from The Australian National University discuss the Engaging Visions project, which took artists into rural communities in the Murray Darling basin. The project saw the artists working with the local communities to develop artworks which highlighted environmental concerns in those communities. The resulting artwork was displayed in the communities they were produced in. A free pdf book of the artwork produced is available at the Engaging Visions website.

    The three-year Engaging Visions project is an initiative of ANU and the Murray Darling Basin Authority. Since 2007, project leaders and a range of artists have engaged with communities in St George (Queensland), Tumut (NSW), Renmark (South Australia) and Benalla (Victoria).

    People from those towns have opened their doors, farm gates and minds, giving artists and researchers insights into how they are balancing environmentally sustainable water practices and broader environmental needs.

    The artists have translated these insights into different media including photography, painting and sculpture. These works have been shown at a series of exhibitions in the communities and many purchased back by the communities they depict.

    For more information see:
    http://engagingvisions.com.au
    http://cpas.anu.edu.au/engagingvisions/

    WIN TV news footage courtesy WIN TV Riverland. With thanks to Jessica Stanley.

    Art Forum: Denise Green

    • by ANUchannel
    • 11 months ago
    • 119 views
    The ANU School of Art and the ACT Government present the 2012 public Art Forum series.

    Denise Green is an Australian-born artist and writer currently based in New York. She is the author of Metonymy in Contemporary Art: A New Paradigm, and her most recent book An Artist's Odyssey has just been released. Denise has a long and distinguished practice and has been included in landmark exhibitions at the Whitney and Guggenheim Museums. Retrospectives of her work have taken place at the Art Gallery of New South Wales, P.S. 1/MoMA in New York and the Museum Kurhaus Kleve (Germany). She was an editor on Semiotext(e) and has written extensively for visual arts journals both internationally and in Australia. In 2007 she was awarded the Order of Australia. In 2012, she will exhibit at Arc One Gallery in Melbourne and the Andrew Baker Gallery in Brisbane.

    School of Art Grad exhibition 2009, ANU (full version)

    • by ANUchannel
    • 3 years ago
    • 894 views
    Graduating artists Heike Qualitz, George Emerson and Tracy Pateman, along with the Head of School Gordon Bull discuss the ANU School of Art Graduate Exhibition 2009.

    This years ANU School of Art Graduate Exhibition features the works of students completing their studies in Bachelor of Visual Arts Honours, Bachelor of Visual Arts, Bachelor of Digital Arts and the Diploma of Art. The exhibition runs from December 5 - 13, 2009.

    Students have majored in wood, textiles, sculpture, printmedia and drawing, photography and media arts, painting, gold and silversmithing, glass and ceramics. Visitors to the School will witness the talent of its emerging artists and experience the quality and diversity of art and craft education offered by the School, from fine furniture making to installation, digital imagery to ceramic objects.

    The works are displayed in the School of Art Gallery, Foyer Gallery, Photospace and in Workshop spaces throughout the School.
