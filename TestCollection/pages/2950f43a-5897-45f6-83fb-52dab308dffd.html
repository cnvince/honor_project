Research interests

Anthropology of governing; postsocialism and postsocialist societies; anthropology of education; processes of subjectification; kinship and gender; language and culture; China; East Asia; USA

Biography

My research involves social, cultural and political change in contemporary China.  I am currently am working on several projects, including a comparative study of urban citizenship in different types of rapidly urbanizing areas in China, a book on urbanization in mid-sized city in Shandong, a series of papers on nation building in China, an edited volume titled Chinese Modernity and the Individual Psyche, and a textbook on Contemporary Chinese Society. Finally, I have recently completed a book on the implications for anthropological theory of issues that arise in the governing of socialist states.  My earlier research examined patterns of gift giving and social exchange in rural China.  I am also co-editor of The China Journal.
