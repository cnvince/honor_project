Dr Kathryn Dwan

PhD
Research Fellow
ANU College of Medicine, Biology and Environment
E: kathryn.dwan@anu.edu.au

Research interests

Medical professionalism, practice nurses, medical informatics, consumer views of health care, social inequality, health workforce, qualitative methods

Biography

Kathryn Dwan (BSc Hons1, BA, PhD) is a health sociologist. Her doctoral work explored the communication difficulties experienced by GPs and Commonwealth health officers during a period of unprecedented engagement between the state and general practice (1998-2006). Since then she has been a chief investigator on the Australian General Practice Nurse Study, which adopted a multi-method approach to understanding the lived experience of practice nurses. With colleagues, she recently completed research on why so many GPs in Canberra choose to work part time and is expanding that interest to understand the issue of part time work from the perspective of full time GPs and practice managers. Kathryn has a growing interest in consumer and community involvement in health policy and practice and, with the Health Care Consumers Association ACT, has undertaken research into consumers’ views on their willingness to see practice nurses. Her particular strengths lie in qualitative research, however, she is currently working with interstate colleagues on the 'Professions in Australia Study' data, which comprises 30 years of longitudinal data about the careers of Australian doctors, engineers and lawyers.

Researcher's projects

Part-time general practitioners in the ACT: How are they perceived by full time GPs and practice managers? (with Kirsty Douglas)

Motivating general practitioners to be involved in medical teaching (with ANU Medical School colleagues)

Interprofessional working: developing and evaluating an educational program to promote teamwork in general practice (with ANU Medical School colleagues)
