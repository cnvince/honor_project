Research interests

Gambling, Mental health, Substance Use & Co-morbidity.  Population Health and Lifecourse Research Perspectives

Biography

Tanya Davidson (formerly Caldwell) undertakes research in the Centre for Gambling Research. She has worked in population health research, investigating gambling, mental health, and substance use, for more than 10 years. She is particuarly interested in the co-occurrence of these behaviours and problems and lifecourse research perspectives.

Researcher's projects

  • The distribution of problem gambling and gambling related harm across the ACT adult population
  • The availability, accessibility and suitability of problem gambling services in the ACT
  • Public attitudes to gambling activities, problems and treatment (in collaboration with ANU Poll)

Current student projects

Elizabeth Kirk: An ethnographic study of gambling in social clubs. This research explores how patrons use social clubs, what they get out of going to the club and being club members.  This includes investigating the ways that club patrons make use of gambling facilities, in particular Electronic Gaming Machines (pokies), that the club provides. This research aims to contribute to understanding the role that gambling and clubs play in people’s lives across the continuum of gambling behaviour.
