H.C. Coombs Creative Arts Fellowship 2010

The Australian Art Orchestra (AAO) together with Indigenous performers from Arnhem Land were the recipients of the 2010 HC Coombs Creative Arts Fellowship at The Australian National University. The Fellowship, awarded each year by the Research School of Humanities and the Arts at ANU, aims to encourage creative arts in Australia through a residency at the University. The AAO - led by renowned jazz artist Paul Grabowsky - brought their collaborative Crossing Roper Bar project to ANU with a series of workshops and a concert at the ANU School of Music.

Crossing Roper Bar

Recipient of the ANU H.C. Coombs Creative Arts Fellowship 2010, the Australian Art Orchestra and the Young Wagilak Group from Ngukurr, created a wonderful marriage of the very old with the very new. Crossing Roper Bar is a celebration of country, of ceremony, and of the power of music to build enduring bridges across cultures, time and space.

Paul Grabowsky Lecture 

Paul Grabowsky presented his lecture "The Complete Musician"  at the School of Music on 14 October. Watch this Lecture

The complete musician by Paul Grabowsky, part of the 2010 H.C. Coombs Creative Arts Fellowship is an examination of the pedagogical needs of elite musicians of the early 21st century and some philosophical observations which underpin these reflections. Mr Paul Grabowsky will challenge tertiary music educators to reflect on their current practice and consider how effective it is in actually preparing students for the careers they will now enter on graduation.

Recognised as Australia’s pre-eminent jazz pianist with over 26 recordings to his name, Mr Grabowsky is regarded as one of Australia’s foremost screen composers with credits for over 17 film and 13 television scores including Human Touch. Innocence, Jessica, The Jungle Book 2, and Shiner. His works for the stage include the opera Love in the Age of Therapy and the multimedia production The Theft of Sita. He has also been involved in Tonight Live and was a composer for the 2000 Sydney Olympics opening and closing ceremonies. In 1994, Mr Grabowsky founded the Australian Art Orchestra and has been its Artistic Director ever since.

Mr Grabowsky began classical piano lessons at age five, studying with Mack Jost. He later began informal studies in jazz, and devoted his energies fully to improvised music from 1978. He lived in Munich, Germany from 1980-1985, where he was active on the local and European jazz scenes, performing and/or recording with Johnny Griffin, Chet Baker, Art Farmer, Benny Bailey, Guenther Klatt, Marty Cook and many others. He has won three ARIA awards, an AFI award, a Helpmann Award and was Sidney Myer Performing Artist of the Year in 2000.

This lecture is awarded to the Australian Art Orchestra and the Young Wagilak Group. The Fellowship is supported by the Research School of Humanities and the Arts and the ANU School of Music.
