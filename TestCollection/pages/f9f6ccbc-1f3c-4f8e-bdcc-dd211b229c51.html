NHMRC Research Fellowships

Those holding any of these Fellowships in the Research School of Biology have ‘lab leader’ status and, as such, receive significant research support from the School. Those coming from outside the School may receive support to help them to establish their research laboratory. All lab leaders are provided with an annual research budget, allocated on the basis of a range of research and teaching indicators. Assessment of research performance includes consideration of the number of papers published in recent years the number of citations attracted by these papers, and the value of the research grants held. Financial support is provided for each PhD and Hons student supervised, and contributions to the undergraduate teaching curriculum are rewarded with additional research support.

In addition to receiving an annual budget allocation based on research and teaching performance, those lab leaders who are ARC and NHMRC Research Fellows but who don’t have a continuing position in the School have returned to them as research support the full amount of the RIBG/JRE earnings that the Fellowship award earns for the School.

Together, these mechanisms deliver significant research funding to high-performing ARC and NHMRC Research Fellows.

With regard to employment conditions, the award of an ARC or NHMRC Research Fellowship does not guarantee the person involved an appointment beyond the tenure of the Fellowship. The ANU College of Medicine, Biology and Environment (of which RSB is a part) does have in place a scheme whereby Research Fellows may be converted to a continuing appointment during, or at the end of, the term of their Fellowship. However it is anticipated that this process will only be used in exceptional circumstances. If the School is in a position to make a continuing appointment the general policy will be to advertise the position internationally, with Fellows in the relevant area encouraged to apply.

Those interested in submitting an application for an ARC or NHMRC Fellowship through the School should contact either the School Director, Kiaran Kirk, or the Head of the relevant Research Division:

Stefan Bröer (Division of Biomedical Science and Biochemistry)

Scott Keogh (Division of Evolution, Ecology and Genetics)

Murray Badger (Division of Plant Sciences)

The Research School of Biology is also seeking to attract ARC Future Fellows, NHMRC Research Fellows, and holders of NHMRC Career Development Awards - learn more here.
