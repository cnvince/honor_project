Research and Work

I am interested in computer system architecture, parallel computing, cluster computing, distributed shared memory systems, as well as direct remote memory access. At August 2011, I submitted my Ph.D thesis "Region-based Techniques for Modeling and Enhancing Cluster OpenMP Performance" to ANU. The thesis was completed in the Computer System research group as a major part of Intel Cluster OpenMP project supervised by Dr. Peter Strazdins and Prof. Alistair Rendell at ANU. At December 2011, I was awarded Ph.D degree in Computer Science at ANU.

An online version of Ph.D. thesis is available at HERE!

I joined ANU Suptercomputer Facility and National Computational Infrastructure at August 2010 as a HPC Consultant and Developer. I am focusing on GPU high performance computing, parallel application (MPI and OpenMP) performance profiling and analysis, and computer system (12000+ CPUs) maintenance. In addition, I am also a co-developer of ANUPBS, a branch of OpenPBS.

After being a pure developer for many years, I joined Microsoft Bing team as a techincal program manager to learn more on leadership and management. Now I spent half of my time on developing data mining, data generation and data processing programs, and another half on designing programs and managing projects. What a nice combination!

I have also got research experience in sensor networks, DSP applications, and computer visualization.
