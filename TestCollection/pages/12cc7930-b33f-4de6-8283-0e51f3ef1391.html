Published on Nov 21, 2012

2012 marks 50 years of European Union and Australian relations. In a series of discussions Ambassadors and/or High Commissioners from EU Member States, together with Australian Ambassadors to Europe and other experts, gather at the ANU Centre for European Studies to discuss why their joined the EU, their current position and how their country interacts with Australia.

In this edition H.E. Mr Noel White, Ambassador for Ireland to Australia and New Zealand, H.E. Mr Rui Quartin-Santos, Ambassador for Portugal to Australia and New Zealand, and Mr Peter Doyle, Assistant Secretary, EU & Western Europe Branch, DFAT take part in discussions addressing the main reasons and benefits of EU membership for Ireland and Portugal. Issues of Eurozone crisis are also addressed in light of the greater internal integration, reassuring a strong political will to further development. H.E. Mr Noel White notes that the EU is not defined by the Eurozone crisis. Further, the cooperation between Australia and Ireland is discussed, touching upon the subjects of bilateral business relations and skilled employment/migration.
