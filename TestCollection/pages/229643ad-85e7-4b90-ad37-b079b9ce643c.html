Research interests

Visual and interactive research methods, action research, participatory research, creative research, gamification, community-based natural resource management, community-based communication, climate change adaptation, indigenous enterprise development, environmental policy

Biography

Lisa is a social scientist with interests at the interface between society and the environment. Through previous research and work Lisa has developed experience in research and communication - relating to sustainable resource management, environmental policy, climate change adaptation and payments for environmental services. This work has been with communities in Australia, SE Asia and Africa. 

Lisa also has a strong interest in visual and participatory research approaches. Her PhD research explored the use of visual methods for the facilitation of collaborative learning and communication in environmental management. Her research was carried out with indigenous people living in protected areas in Arnhem Land and Vietnam.

Currently, Lisa is working with the School of Sociology (ANU) as a Postdoctoral Fellow on research relating to food security and community-based adaptation to climate change.
