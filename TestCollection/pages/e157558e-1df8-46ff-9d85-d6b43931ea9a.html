SCULPTURE: Place and Space

in
  • Symposium
Date: 
Fri, 10 May 2013 (All day) - Sun, 12 May 2013 (All day)
Venue: 
James O Fairfax Theatre and Gandel Hall, National Gallery of Australia

This symposium is a collaboration between the Australian National University, Research School of Humanities and the Arts, the ANU School of Art and the National Gallery of Australia.

The symposium is a highlight of the 2013 Centenary of Canberra program TOUCH: Sculpture and the Land that has been designed to celebrate sculpture in all its forms.

TOUCH: Sculpture and the Land is taking place in venues across Canberra in May 2013 and includes special exhibitions at the ANU Drill Hall Gallery and School of Art Gallery, various activities and events at ACT Galleries and Art Centres, artists in residence, walks, talks and tours of sculpture collections on the ANU Campus and International Sculpture Park and the NGA Sculpture Garden. Opportunities to visit Canberra’s public sculpture collections in Civic, Parliament House, New Acton and the Australian War Memorial are also being arranged.

TOUCH: Sculpture and the Land Symposium

in
  • Symposium
Date: 
Fri, 17 May 2013 (All day) - Mon, 20 May 2013 (All day)
Venue: 
National Gallery of Australia

Public art has long played a prominent role in Canberra’s cultural landscape. Over the past three decades, the ANU School of Art has played an important part in this development with its Sculpture Workshop, exhibitions and Visiting Artists in Residence.

The Symposium will examine the work, the people and the conversation around sculpture in Canberra and set it in a national/international context. Themes will elaborate on issues raised in the exhibitions, the history with commissioned permanent and temporary work for public spaces, environmental considerations, community interaction with the wide range of contemporary sculpture activity and its role in the creation of urban spaces and stimulating public imagination.

New Directions in the Literature of Ideas: A 1 day symposium

in
  • Symposium
Date: 
Fri, 02 Nov 2012, 9:00am - 6:00pm
Venue: 
AD Hope Conference Room, Level 1

Convenors: Dr Ned Curthoys or Dr Shameem Black

Particularly since the publication of J.M. Coetzee’s Elizabeth Costello (2003) with its searing critique of the industrial slaughter of animals, there has been a renewed focus on the novel of ideas or ‘academic novel.’

This one day interdisciplinary symposium explores literature’s capacity to provoke and reconfigure debate in the public sphere about issues of contemporary salience such as animal rights, climate change, and the political effects of collective memory. It will interrogate the relationship between literature and philosophy, reflecting on Martha Nussbaum’s argument that literature can offer a sensate and imaginative form of moral philosophy. The conference is of interest to staff and postgraduates in disciplines including English, history, philosophy, and political science.

All welcome, no RSVP necessary.

The Projected Image Heritage of Australia and New Zealand

in
  • Symposium
Date: 
Fri, 14 Dec 2012 (All day)
Venue: 
ANU School of Art

A symposium to scope future research into magic lantern slide projection in Australia and New Zealand.

Join colleagues from the National Film and Sound Archive, the ANU, Melbourne University, Sydney University, Griffith University and other institutions to catch up on the research currently being done, and to scope the potential for further research and collaboration.

For over a century Australia’s visual culture was profoundly shaped by the technology of magic-lantern slide projection. From the 1840s to the 1930s this technology permeated theaters, lecture halls, church services, private homes and even open public spaces. It crossed from popular entertainment venues to elite educational institutions, and evolved from beautifully hand-painted slides to high-quality photographic reproductions.

Happiness and Pain: Cross-Cultural and Cross-Linguistic Perspectives

in
  • Symposium
Date: 
Fri, 28 Sep 2012, 9:00am - Sat, 29 Sep 2012, 1:00pm
Venue: 
Scarth Room, University House, ANU

“Happiness” and “pain” matter to everyone, but how much do ways of talking and thinking about happiness and pain vary across cultures and epochs of history?

Both themes – “happiness” and “pain” – are at the forefront of current research and cross-disciplinary debates across a range of disciplines, including linguistics, anthropology, psychology, psychiatry, cultural and communication studies and others. They are also of central interest to practical areas such as nursing, counselling and palliative care.

Artworlds Symposium

in
  • Symposium
Date: 
Sat, 15 Sep 2012, 9:00am - 5:00pm
Venue: 
School of Cultural Inquiry Conference Room, first floor, AD Hope Building (14)

The School of Cultural Inquiry will hold the 2012 Art History and Curatorship Postgraduate Symposium on Saturday 15 September.  It will be a full day’s program with lunch, morning and afternoon teas provided.

The guiding theme—Artworlds—while allowing for any field of art history research, is also there to remind the presenters and the audience there is not just one art world, rather a multiplicity of art worlds—sometimes intersecting, sometimes not—all expressing different views of the world.  We hope to attract a diverse group of papers designed to create stimulating discussion in a relaxed, but professional, environment where postgraduates and early career researchers can present original ideas to a supportive audience.
