Published on May 20, 2013

In this extensive interview, ANU alumna and well-known human rights lawyer Jennifer Robinson discusses some of the key human rights challenges around the globe.

She talks about Australia's need to speak out about Indonesian policies in West Papua, her experiences working on the Bradley Manning case in the United States and her time representing WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange.

Robinson also outlines why she thinks it is essential that young people learn languages from the Asia Pacific region in what is being labelled the 'Asian Century', as well as how her experiences studying in Indonesia as an undergraduate inspired her remarkable career.

Jennifer Robinson graduated from ANU with a combined degree in Asian studies and law and was named joint ANU Young Alumnus of the Year 2013. Robinson is also the face of the ANU Asia and the Pacific Travel Grant.

For more information visit the ANU College of Asia and the Pacific:
http://asiapacific.anu.edu.au

This video accompanies a story in the Winter 2013 edition of ANU Reporter magazine: http://news.anu.edu.au/anu-reporter/
