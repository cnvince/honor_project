Dr Kim Huynh

BA (Hons) PhD International Relations
Lecturer in Politics and International Relations
ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences
E: kim.huynh@anu.edu.au
Flag this profile

Areas of expertise

  • International Relations 160607
  • Political Theory And Political Philosophy 160609

Research interests

Forced migration, children and armed conflict, political literature and East-West relations.

Biography

Kim Huynh teaches courses in refugee politics, political philosophy and international relations and is a recipient of the ANU Vice Chancellor's Award for Excellence in Teaching. His biography of his parents, Where the Sea Takes Us (Fourth Estate, 2008), has attracted academic and popular acclaim. It was short-listed for the Australian Society of Author’s and ACT best book of 2007. He is the 2013 winner of the Society for Humanistic Anthropology’s fiction award. Kim co-edited The Culture Wars: Australian and American Politics in the 21st Century (Palgrave-McMillan, 2009) and has published journal articles and book chapters in the fields of political theory, women’s studies, forced migration and ethnology. He is working on a collection of stories about Vietnamese youth and a monograph entitled, The West: A guide for perplexed Asians. He has also written essays and articles for a range of Australian newspapers and the BBC Vietnamese.  

Researcher's projects

Kim Huynh is currently working on the following projects:

- a co-authored book on children in conflict zones;

- an Asialink funded collection of short stories about young people in Vietnam;

- an artsACT funded biography of a Sudanese child soldier and refugee; and

- a book about political and cultural relations between Asia and the West.

Current student projects

Past and Current Student Research Topics: Australian political and economic crises; refugees and migration; political philosophy; children and armed conflict; Vietnam/China relations, Asia and the West.
