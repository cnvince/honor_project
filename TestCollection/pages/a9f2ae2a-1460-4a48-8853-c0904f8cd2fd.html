Dr Nathan Harris

Community Capacity Building in Child Protection Projects (CCB)
ANU College of Asia and the Pacific
E: nathan.harris@anu.edu.au
Flag this profile
Jump to: Biography|Publications|Related websites

Biography

I am currently a Fellow at the Regulatory Institutions Network (RegNet). Previously I have been a Lecturer at the Institute of Criminology, University of Cambridge, and a Post-Doctoral Fellowship at the Katholieke Universiteit Leuven, Belgium

My current project explores the experiences of parents withing child protection systems and the impact of these experiences on their future capacity to care for their children. This is an extension of previous research which draws on perspectives from a number of academic disciplines to examine how institutions, such as criminal justice, can more effectively respond to social problems. Restorative justice and responsive regulation are a focus of this research because they represent important frameworks for reform. The psychological dynamics between social disapproval and the shame-related emotions are important because they explain how individuals respond to intervention in their lives, and why some some approaches at intervention are better at creating change than others.
