I am a Research Fellow in the Department of Quantum Science at the Australian National University. I obtained my PhD at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ) in 2002, investigating the effects of decoherence in the quantum-classical transition for chaotic systems and also the use of decoherence for quantum state control. I then spent three years at the Max-Planck Institute for the Physics of Complex Systems where I worked on the theory of entanglement measures and its dynamics in open quantum systems.
By the end of 2005 I moved to Australia where I took my present position at the ANU. I am interested in the dynamics of monitored quantum systems, quantum feedback control and stochastic simulations. I have been particularly involved in the use of measurement and feedback techniques to control quantum states with applications in quantum information and atom optics problems.
