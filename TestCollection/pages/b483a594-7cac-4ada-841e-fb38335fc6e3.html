Dr Chantal Crozet

Lecturer (French Studies)
ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences
E: Chantal.Crozet@anu.edu.au
Flag this profile

Areas of expertise

  • French Language 200306
  • Applied Linguistics And Educational Linguistics 200401

Research interests

Intercultural Language Education; The Philosophical, Ethical & Spiritual Dimensions of Language Education; Second Culture & Language Acquisition; Language, Culture and Identity Politics; The Teaching of French language & Culture; French Songs as Historical & Socio-political Discourse.

Publications

  • Crozet, C 2009, 'L'espace FLE australien: lieu pluriel, confrontations de cultures et besoin de mediation (the place of French as a foreign language in Australia: a space of plurality, cultural conflict and need of mediation)', Le Francais dans le Monde, vol. 46, pp. 166-175.
  • Crozet, C 2008, 'Australia's Linguistic Culture and Its Impact on Languages Education', Babel, vol. 42, no. 3, pp. 19-24.
  • Crozet, C 2008, ILTLP Intercultural Language Teaching and Learning in Practice - Final Project Report, Research Centre for Languages and Cultures, University of South Australia.
  • Crozet, C 2007, 'Book review: Language, Society and Consciousness. Edited by R. Hasan, Jonathan J. Webster', Australian Review of Applied Linguistics (print edition), vol. 30.
  • Crozet, C 2006, 'The Spiritual Dimension of Intercultural Language Education', International Journal of the Humanities, vol. 4, no. 3, pp. 119-124.
  • Crozet, C 2003, 'A conceptual framework to help teachers identify where culture is located in language use', in Joseph Lo Bianco and Chantal Crozet (ed.), Teaching Invisible Culture: Classroom Practice and Theory, Language Australia, Melbourne, pp. 39-49.
  • Crozet, C & Maurer, L 2003, 'Teaching French culture in language use', in Joseph Lo Bianco and Chantal Crozet (ed.), Teaching Invisible Culture: Classroom Practice and Theory, Language Australia, Melbourne, pp. 119-145.
  • Brown, P, Crozet, C, Maurer, L et al 2002, 'French in Australia: Policies and Practices', in Kamal Salhi (ed.), French in and out of Australia: Language Policies, Intercultural Antagonisms and Dialogue, Peter Lang Publishing Group, Bern, pp. 266-290.
  • Crozet, C 2001, 'Characteristics of Indigenous Languages and Cultures, Part 2', UNESCO Regional Workshop on Functional Literacy for Indigenous Peoples, ed. Dave Tout, Language Australia, Melbourne, Victoria, pp. 3-5, 10.
  • Crozet, C & Liddicoat, A 2001, 'Acquiring French interactional norms through instruction', in Rose, Kenneth R and Kasper, Gabriele (ed.), Pragmatics in Language Teaching, Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, UK, pp. 125-144.
  • Crozet, C & Liddicoat, A 2000, 'Teaching culture as an integrated part of language: implications for the aims, approaches and pedagogies of language teaching', in Crozet, C.$; Liddicoat, A.$ (ed.), Teaching languages, Teaching Cultures, Language Australia, Melbourne, pp. 1-18.
  • Liddicoat, A & Crozet, C, eds, 2000, Teaching Languages, Teaching Cultures, Language Australia, Melbourne.
  • Crozet, C & Liddicoat, A 1999, 'Turning promises into practices: The challenges of intercultural language teaching', Australian Language Matters, vol. 7, no. 1, pp. 3-4, 12.
  • Crozet, C & Liddicoat, A 1999, 'The challenge of intercultural language teaching: Engaging with culture in the classroom', in Lo Biano, J. (ed.), Striving for the Third Place: Intercultural Competence through Language Education, Language Australia, Melbourne, pp. 113-125.
  • Crozet, C, Liddicoat, A & Lo Bianco, J 1999, 'Intercultural competence: From language policy to language education', in Lo Biano, J. (ed.), Striving for the Third Place: Intercultural Competence through Language Education, Language Australia, Melbourne, pp. 1-20.
  • Brown, P, Crozet, C & Trevitt, A 1999, 'L'enseignement a distance en Australie: Au-dela de le'enjeu geographique', Revue internationale d'education, vol. 23, pp. 141-147.
