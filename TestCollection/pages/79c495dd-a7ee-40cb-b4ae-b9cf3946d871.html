Dr Fubao Sun

Hydrologist, PhD (D. Eng.)
Research Fellow (Academic Level B)
ANU College of Medicine, Biology and Environment and ANU College of Physical and Mathematical Sciences
E: fubao.sun@anu.edu.au

Research interests

Seek a simple physical-statistical understanding of (1) the complicated interplay between ecologic, hydrologic and climatic aspects of the changing environment and (2) its role in governing the water and energy budgets over a spectrum of spatial-temporal scales, and (3) further investigations on influences of climate change to hydraulic and water resources engineering.

Hydroclimate Variability vs Change:

Evaporation paradox, hydrologic cycle & climate, multi-model ensemble of Global Climate Models, statistical downscaling

Hydraulic and Water Resources Engineering:

Global/regional water and energy balance, climate change and water resources, extreme rainfall & hydraulic engineering design.

Ecohydrology:

Coupled water, heat and carbon cycles --model and field experiment, stochastic water balance model

Statistical Analysis:

Analysis of variance, time series analysis, signal vs noise, stochastic modeling

Biography

Fubao (1980-) is a Research Fellow in CoE for Climate System Science at ANU since 2011.

He was (2007-2011) a Postdoc at ANU and in Oki-Kanae Global Hydrology Group at Univ of Tokyo. He obtained BE and DE in Hydralic Engineering at Tsinghua Univ(1998-2007), awarded Outstanding Graduate and the Best PhD Thesis (Top 20, department's 1st time).

He won twice the 3rd prize at National Math Competition (1995, 1997), Outstanding Student of China by BanYueTan Magazine (1997), was a Champion of 400m Race at Tsinghua Univ Sports Meeting (2001) and a Marathon Runner in Beijing (2003) and Tokyo (2007).

He recently received Editors' Choice Award (1%) of Water Resour Res. His WRR 2011, GRL 2012 were featured as AGU Research Spotlights. He is an Editor of J Geo-disasters (Springer)
