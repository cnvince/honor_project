Biography

Daniel Shaddock received his PhD from the Australian National University in 2001 for work on interferometry for gravitational wave detection. In 2002 he joined the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) as a National Research Council post-doctoral fellow before becoming a JPL employee later that year. His research at JPL focused on the Laser Interferometer Space Antenna where he was appointed a JPL Director’s Fellow. From 2007 to 2010 he divided his time between JPL and ANU. He is now a Future Fellow at the Australian National University in the Department of Quantum Science.
