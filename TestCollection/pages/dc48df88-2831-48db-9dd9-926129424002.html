WANDERING ISLANDS: A.D. Hope and Australian Poetry

Dates: 17-20 July 2007

Venue: Old Canberra House, ANU, Canberra

Convener:
Associate Professor David Brooks
English Department, University of Sydney

Marking the centenary of the birth of one of Australia’s most significant poets, this conference will examine the life, work and influence of A.D. Hope with three days of papers and readings from major Australian and international scholars and poets, one day devoted to discussions of Hope’s work, another to Hope’s place in Australian and international poetry, and a third to biographical concerns.

Keynote speaker: Kevin Hart (Notre Dame, U.S.A.); Ann McCulloch (Deakin); and Didier Coste (Sfax [Tunis] and Michel de Montaine [Bordeaux])

The conference will follow almost immediately a two-day colloquium (13th and 14 July) at the University of Sydney on the work of Christopher Brennan and Stéphane Mallarmé.
