Biography

Hal Hill is the H.W. Arndt Professor of Southeast Asian Economies, Arndt Corden Department of Economics, Crawford School of Economics and Government, College of Asia and the Pacific, Australian National University.

His research interests are the economies of ASEAN, especially Indonesia, Malaysia, The Philippines and the transition economies; industrialization and foreign investment in East Asia; regional (sub-national) development dynamics; and Australia’s economic relations with the Asia-Pacific region. He also teaches a course on the Southeast Asian economies.

He is the author or editor of 15 books and has written about 140 academic papers and book chapters. Major titles include The Indonesian Economy since 1966 (Cambridge University Press, 2nd edition, 2000) and The Philippine Economy (co-edited with Arsenio Balisacan, Oxford University Press, 2003). Since 2000, he has also co-edited books on East Timor development issues, the social impacts of the Asian economic crisis, regional development dynamics in the Philippines, foreign investment and globalization in developing Asian economies, East Asia’s high-tech drive, and the challenge of ‘graduation’ for Malaysia.

From 1986 to 1998 he headed the University’s Indonesia Project, and for most of that time edited the Bulletin of Indonesian Economic Studies. He is an occasional oped contributor to Australian and Asian newspapers. He has worked as a consultant for the Australian Government, the Indonesian Government, the World Bank, the Asian Development Bank, and several United Nations agencies. He has held various visiting appointments and is on the editorial board of 13 academic journals.
