Dr Sally K. May

PhD (ANU); BA (Hons.) (Flinders Uni)
Senior Lecturer, School of Archaeology and Anthropology & Rock Art Research Centre

E: sally.may@anu.edu.au

Research interests

Indigenous archaeology, rock art studies, cultural heritage management, archaeology of art, museum studies, cross-cultural encounters, anthropology of collecting.

Current field research areas: Arnhem Land (Australia), Kakadu National Park, Yunnan Province (China).

Biography

Sally K. May is convenor of the Graduate Program in Liberal Arts (Cultural and Environmental Heritage) and Senior Lecturer in the School of Archaeology and Anthropology. Sally's PhD was in interdisciplinary cross-cultural research and focused on the role of art and community art centres in remote Indigenous Australian communities.

Cultural and Environmental Heritage Graduate Program (ANU): http://rsha.anu.edu.au/liberal-arts/heritage

Researcher's projects

Mirarr Rock Art Project

The Mirarr Rock Art Project (2011 – 2015) aims to document rock art sites on Mirarr land and is a partnership between the Gundjeihmi Aboriginal Corporation and the Australian National University.

Picturing Change: 21st century perspectives on recent Australian rock art

ARC Discovery Project (Chief Investigations: Prof. Paul S.C. Tacon, Dr. Sally K. May, Dr. June Ross, Dr. Alistair Paterson). In this five year project (2007-2011) contact period rock art from across Australia is being documented, with fieldwork in western Arnhem Land (NT), Wollemi National Park (NSW) and central Australia, west of Alice Springs (NT).

Kunwinjku Language Project

This project was initiated in 2007 and is funded by the Federal Government program 'Maintenance of Indigenous Language and Records' through the West Arnhem Land Shire Council. The project was established in order to strengthen and promote the cultural identity of Kunwinjku language speakers in Arnhem Land through activiites relating to language and culture. For further information please contact: Dr. Murray Garde (mgarde3@bigpond.com) or visit: http://www.kunwinjku.org/.

Available student projects
