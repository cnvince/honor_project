Biography

Dr Phyll Dance RN, RM, BA (ANU), PhD (ANU)

Phyll has been working in the alcohol and other drugs area for the past 22 years. Outreach activities have included: volunteer work at the AIDS Action Council of the ACT Inc; membership of Steering Committees for the Australian Injecting and Illicit Drug Users’ League, and for Canberra Alliance for Harm Minimisation and Advocacy; being on the Board of the Alcohol and Drug Foundation of the ACT (Inc); and Membership of an Australian Therapeutic Communities Association Steering Committee.

For the past 11 years Phyll has held research and teaching positions at the National Centre for Epidemiology and Population Health at the Australian National University. During this time she has largely focused on projects related to Indigenous health. This work has always been conducted in collaboration with Indigenous organisations and individuals, and has always included two-way transfer of skills. Projects have included: research on bloodborne viruses; problematic alcohol use; investigations of the service and treatment needs of Elders, and of Indigenous people who use illegal drugs; and estimates of the number of Indigenous people in the ACT and region using heroin.

 Since April 2010, Phyll has also been employed at the Australian Institute of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health.

Phyll established the Indigenous Health Interest Group at the College of Medicine, Biology and the Environment in 2008 and has continued to be an active member of the Group. She is also involved in developing the College’s Reconciliation Action Plan. In 2010 Phyll was presented with the inaugural Vice Chancellor's Award for Reconcilliarion.
