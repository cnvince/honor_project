Published on May 21, 2013

In this video, four experts from the ANU Strategic and Defence Studies Centre examine the nation's latest security and defence policy - the 2013 Defence white paper.

Featuring some of Australia's top defence thinkers, including past White Papers authors, this panel will discuss the new Defence White Paper, what it says, the judgements and claims it makes about Australia's security environment and defence capabilities, as well as what these panelists believe should have also been included.

Speakers include:
Paul Dibb AM, Emeritus Professor of Strategic Studies, former Deputy Secretary of Defence and author of the 1986 Dibb review on Australia's Defence Capabilities.

Hugh White, Professor of Strategic Studies, former Deputy Secretary for Strategy and Intelligence, and author of the 2000 Defence White Paper.

Dr Richard Brabin-Smith AO, ANU visiting fellow, and former Deputy Secretary for Strategic Policy and Chief Defence Scientist.

Admiral Chris Barrie AC, ANU visiting fellow and Chief of the Defence Force from 1998 till 2002.

This panel was hosted by the Strategic and Defence Studies Centre in the ANU College of Asia and the Pacific. For more insight, analysis and commentary on strategy and defence visit http://asiapacific.anu.edu.au
