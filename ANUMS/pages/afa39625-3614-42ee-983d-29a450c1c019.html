Entry 29 Founder Series Fireside Chats
Aug 15 @ 5:00 pm – 6:30 pm

You are invited to attend the next event in the Entry 29 Founders Series ‘Fireside chats’. Our speaker this time is Sylvia Tulloch.

Sylvia Tulloch was the Founder of Dyesol (ASX:DYE)which she listed on the ASX in 2005. While she was MD the company reached a market capitalisation of over $170 million. She started life as a materials scientist with many years’ experience in the establishment and management of high technology business with a particular interest in the commercialisation process and the clean technology sector. She is an angel investor in and Director of unlisted companies including Uniflow Power, commercialising next generation steam engines and Perimeter Security Industries, commercialising fibre optic security systems.

 

Come and hear Sylvia talk about her entrepreneurial journey. Spaces are limited for this free event so please register early!

 

DATE:  Thursday 15 August 2013

TIME:  Arrive at 5pm for a 5:15pm start (end 6:15pm)

VENUE: Entry 29 – Seminar Room – 17a Childers Street, ACTON, ACT (Next to The Street Theatre)

COST: FREE

REGISTER: Click here to register.

ANU Entrepreneurs Pitching Event
Aug 16 @ 6:00 pm – 8:00 pm

The ANU Entrepreneurs Society is holding it’s Annual Pitching Competition on Friday the 16th of August. This is a great event to put forward a start-up idea to a panel of expert judges or to simply practice your pitching skills.

You can come as a team or as an individual and no preparation is required. The best part is the winners receive $400 and there are also prizes for 2nd and 3rd.

To sign up (this is a free event) please register on Eventbrite.

For more info please visit the ANU Entrepreneurs’ event page.

Introduction to Importing
Aug 20 @ 8:30 am – 12:30 pm

Are you importing, or contemplating starting importing for your business?
If so this course is for you!

It will provide the essentials for a successful importing business including:

  • How to structure your import pricing
  • Managing the import process
  • Ensuring legal and IP considerations are covered
  • Getting what you have ordered
  • Understanding the payment options and risks
  • Role of the Customs Broker.

This workshop is presented by Peter Mace from the Export Council of Australia.

Go to Canberra BusinessPoint page for more information and to register.

The Australian Innovation Challenge Awards
Aug 26 all-day

If you have a great idea with the power to affect people’s health, wealth or environment, you should enter the $70,000 The Australian Innovation Challenge awards.
You can enter whether you’re a professional scientist or engineer, an educator, an innovator in community services or a creative genius inventing in your shed.
Submit your innovation for an opportunity to be recognised by the nation’s leading daily newspaper and website, and for an opportunity to win a share of $70,000 in prize money.

Entries close on the 26 August 2013.

For more information, check the Australian Innovation Challenge website.
