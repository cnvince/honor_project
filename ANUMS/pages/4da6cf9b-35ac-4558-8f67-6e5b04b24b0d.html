Professor Greg Stuart gives this lecture entitled 'Fundamental Mechanisms Underlying Synaptic Integration' at The Australian National University on 30 August 2010. This talk was given at The John Curtin School of Medical Research at ANU.

After completing his PhD at ANU in 1991, Greg Stuart undertook postdoctoral studies at the Max Planck Institute fur medizinische Forschung, Heidelberg, Germany, before returning to ANU in 1996.

He has received a number of awards including an Alexander von Humboldt Fellowship in 1991, the AW Campbell Award in 1998, and the Sofja Kovalevskaja Prize in 2002,

His research investigates synaptic integration, and the processes used to generate the action potential. Professor Stuart is the Head of the Department of Neuroscience at ANU.
