Dr Ariane Utomo

BAS, BEc (Hons), PhD (ANU)
Research Fellow
ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences
E: Ariane.Utomo@anu.edu.au
Flag this profile

Research interests

Gender and labour market:

  • Women as secondary earners: gendered labour market expectations in urban Indonesia
  • The gender wage gap (Greater Jakarta Transition to Adulthood Project)

Workforce ageing:

  • Worklife table in Australian organisations
  • Work and exit patterns of older workers in Australian public sectors
  • Gender and employment separtion patterns of older workers in Australia

Online social networks:

  • Online social capital: Facebook and income generation among married women in urban Indonesia
  • Online social networks and young people in Greater Jakarta (Greater Jakarta Transition to Adulthood Project

 

Biography

Ariane completed her PhD with the Australian Demographic and Social Research Institute of the ANU in 2008. Her PhD thesis examines the notion of women as secondary earners  and the interplay between gender role expectations and gendered labour market outcomes in contemporary Indonesia.

Prior to commencing her PhD, she was a Monbusho Research Exchange Scholar at the Graduate School of Economics of Chuo University, Japan in 2000/2002. She continued to pursue her research interest in ADSRI, joining as an ANU Summer Research Scholar, and working on special tabulations from the 2000 Indonesian Population Census under the supervision of Terry Hull in summer 2002/2003.

Ariane is currently involved in an ARC Linkage project on workforce ageing in Australia with Jeromey Temple and Peter McDonald. She is also teaching Education and Labour Force (DEMO8045), and has been involved in co-lecturing/tutoring for Mathematics for Social Scientsts (DEMO6926), Computing for Social Scientists (DEMO6927), Population and Society (POPS2001), Population and Australia (SOCY2032) , Gender and Population (DEMO8048), Demographic Analysis II(DEMO8011), and Survey Data Analysis II (DEMO8089).
