Biography

Dr Olsen is a NHMRC post-doctoral fellow at the Kirby Institute, UNSW and a Visiting Fellow at the NCECH, ANU. With over 10 years of involvement in illicit drug use research and policy her research interests include drug use, hepatitis B and C, sexual and reproductive health, social inequalities of health and Indigenous health. Her theoretical background lies in the anthropology of health and illness, qualitative research methods and social science in public health. Her current research includes: healthcare needs of women with hepatitis C; pathways to treatment for Indigenous Australian's with hepatitis B; and Implementing Expanded Naloxone Availability in the ACT (I-ENAACT) program evaluation.

Researcher's projects

- The effects of opioids on hormone levels of women with hepatitis C: an exploratory study

- Independent evaluation of the 'Expanded Naloxone Availability in the ACT Program

Current student projects

ANUMS PEP Project 2013

  • Aims + Objectives
    • To examine the background literature on medical professionals and medical students attitudes towards people who use illicit drugs.
    • To measure the current and continuing attitudes of ANU Medical School students towards people who use illicit drugs via the development of a pilot survey. The survey will measure student attitudes towards illicit drugs, people who use illicit drugs, education on this topic (present and future) and intentions to work in drug and alcohol medicine. A full study proposal will be compiled.
    •  To scope and review the ANU Medical School’s current curriculum content on illicit drug use. A search will also be conducted on national and state-based medical education standards/guidelines (if such exists). A report detailing the scoping review will be compiled for the ANU Drug and Alcohol Clinic.
