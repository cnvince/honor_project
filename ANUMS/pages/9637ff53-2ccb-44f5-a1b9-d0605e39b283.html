Dr Matthew Gray

Associate Professor
ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences
E: m.gray@anu.edu.au
Flag this profile

  • Political Science 1606
  • International Relations 160607
  • International Business 150308
  • Tourism 1506

Research interests

The politics, political economy, international relations, and business dynamics of the Middle East, especially the Arab world.

Biography

Employment:

  • Associate Professor, Centre for Arab and Islamic Studies, ANU, Canberra - 3 January 2013 - present.
  • Senior Lecturer, Centre for Arab and Islamic Studies, ANU, Canberra - 10 January 2005 - 2 January 2013.
  • Director, International Cooperation Branch, Department of Immigration and Multicultural and Indigenous Affairs, Canberra - April 2004 - January 2005.
  • Assistant Director, Department of Defence, Canberra, August 2002 - April 2004.
  • Various roles, Australian Trade Commission, Canberra and Milan, August 1997 - August 2002.

Qualifications:

  • PhD, ANU, Canberra, 2000 (thesis on the political economy of tourism in the Middle East)
  • MA, Macquarie University, Sydney, 1994 (thesis on democratization in Yemen)
  • BA, Macquarie University, Sydney, 1992

Researcher's projects

  • The politics, political economy, and international relations of Qatar;
  • The security dynamics and the foreign-domestic policy linkages of Saudi Arabia;
  • The political economy of tourism in the Middle East, especially in the Arab Gulf states and Iraq, and including tourism and development issues and Gulf air and rail issues;
  • The political economy of the foreign policies and foreign relations of the Arab states of the Gulf; and
  • Conspiracy theories and political language in the Arab world.

Available student projects

Anything on the contemporary (post-nineteenth century) Middle East, but especially topics on political science, political economy, international relations, or certain business/management issues in the region, and especially the Arab countries and within that, the Gulf.

Current student projects

Adel Abdel Ghafar, A Political Economy of the 2011 Egyptian Revolution; Jessie Moritz, Revising Rentier Theory for Modern Arab Gulf States; Heather Yeates, The Gulf and Syria Relations; Jerry Knockles, The Constitution of Crisis: An Empirical Study of US Decision-Making on Intervention in Middle East Crises; Adrian d’Hagé, The Influence of Religion on US Foreign Policy in the Middle East; Izzat Abdulhadi, Palestinian Civil Society and NGO Groups (MPhil).

Past student projects

Erika Schwarze, Palestinian Public Opinion Polling: The Impact of the People’s Voice on Decision-Making in a Transitional Democracy (awarded 2012); Dr Justin Leach, A Comparison of Sudan’s Post-Independence Peace Accords (awarded 2010); Dr Richard Scrivener, Australian Iranian Relations and the Australian-US Alliance: 1979 to 2003 (awarded 2010); Dr Sarah Phillips, The Resilience of Authoritarianism in a Pluralised Arab State: A Case Study of Yemen (awarded 2007); Anne-Sophie Gintzburger, The Sources in Variation in the Application of Shari’a-Compliant Finance Contracts: A Comparative Analysis of Shari’a Scholars, Shari’a Board Roles, and Regulatory Variations in Malaysia and the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) States (MPhil; awarded 2010).
