Research interests

Biography

Dr Christopher Vas is the Policy Research Program Leader at the HC Coombs Policy Forum – Crawford School of Public Policy at the Australian National University (ANU). He leads the Productivity in the Public and Private Sectors, and Policy Futures program at the Forum. He has extensive experience working on public policy issues, research, analysis, and its implementation with expertise in innovation and productivity policy, labor market and public management.

Dr Vas was co-chair of the Trans-Pacific dialogue 'Creating a Productive Future: Social and Economic Challenges, Policy and Governance' jointly hosted by the Crawford School of Public Policy (ANU) and Harvard Kennedy School (Harvard University). He's also the co-author of 'Demystifying Productivity for Better-Informed Policy'.

Previously, Dr Vas was Assistant Director with the Australian Government’s Department of Education, Employment and Workplace Relations (DEEWR) where he advised on issues relating to human capital, workplace innovation and industrial productivity and was responsible for the provision of evidence-based policy advice.

Dr. Vas also spent time at the Australian Federal Police (AFP) as Higher Education and Research Manager and Senior Policy Adviser. He played an instrumental role in establishing AFP’s partnership with the Research Centre of Excellence in Policing and Security. He was AFP’s representative on the Dept of Prime Minister & Cabinet's National Security Science & Technology research panel. 

Dr Vas also held positions of international repute including at the world-leading Indian School of Business. 

Dr Vas holds a Doctorate in Public Policy (PhD) from the ANU having studied the role of university-based think tanks, a Master of Business Administration (MBA). He was also visiting researcher at the Stanford University, Harvard University and University of Wisconsin-Madison. He was the recipient of the Young Investigators Grant award in 2008 and a Public Administration Grant in 2010.
