Dr Benjamin Schreer

MA (Kiel), PhD (Kiel)
Deputy Head and Senior Lecturer, Strategic and Defence Studies Centre
ANU College of Asia and the Pacific
E: benjamin.schreer@anu.edu.au
Flag this profile

Areas of expertise

  • Political Science 1606

Research interests

Australian strategic and defence policy; NATO; US Asia-Pacific strategy; counterinsurgency

Biography

Dr Benjamin Schreer is a Senior Lecturer in SDSC’s Graduate Studies in Strategy & Defence Program and managing editor of the journal Security Challenges. He received a PhD in Political Science from Kiel University, working on Australia’s strategic policy. He also received a Master of Political Science from Kiel University, and studied international relations and security studies at Coventry University. Before joining SDSC in 2010 he was Deputy Director of the Aspen Institute Germany (2009-2010), Research Group Leader at the University of Konstanz (2008-2009), and Research Fellow at the German Institute for International and Security Studies [Stiftung Wissenschaft und Politik, SWP] in Berlin, Germany (2003-2008).

He is a member of the International Institute for Strategic Studies (IISS) in London; an associate fellow in the "NATO in a Changing World' program of the Norwegian Institute for Defence Studies; and a member of the board of trustees of the Cologne Forum for International Relations and Security Policy.

Researcher's projects

  • Future of US Strategy in Southeast Asia (book project)
  • The Future of US Military Presence in the Asia-Pacific (book project)
  • 2012 Visiting Fellowship Program on 'Power and Polarity: The ascent and descent of Great Powers' of the Nobel Institute in Oslo, Norway (May 2012)
