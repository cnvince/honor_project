Ms Vivien Holmes

BA/LLB (Hons)(1984); Grad Dip, Public Sector Admin.(1995); MSoc.Sc.(Int'l Dev't (2006)
Senior Lecturer
ANU College of Law
E: holmesv@law.anu.edu.au
Flag this profile

Areas of expertise

  • Legal Practice, Lawyering And The Legal Profession 180121

Research interests

Vivien's research interests include legal ethics, legal practice and the legal profession.

Biography

Vivien is a Senior Lecturer in the ANU College of Law.  She teaches Legal Ethics in the ANU Legal Workshop.  Prior to joining ANU, Vivien worked as a litigation solicitor in private and government practice, a government legal policy officer, the Registrar of the NT Supreme Court, the NT Registrar of Probates, the NT Deputy Coroner and a Judicial Registrar of the NT Magistrates' Court. She has been a member of the Social Security Appeals Tribunal.

Vivien Masters' thesis was on Domestic Violence Law Reform in Fiji. 
  .

Researcher's projects

Vivien is researching (together with colleagues from the ANU College of Law, Tony Foley, Margie Rowe and Stephen Tang ) the transiiton of law graduates from student to professional lawyer.

VIvien is enrolled in a PhD and is researching the professional challenges faced by inhouse counsel working for corporations that invest in developing countries.
