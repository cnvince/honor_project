Dr Roald Maliangkaij

ANU College of Asia and the Pacific

Research interests

South Korea's cultural industries and cultural policy, music, folklore, cinema, consumer culture, advertising, magazines, and fashion accessories

Biography

After I finished my PhD on the preservation of folksongs in South Korea, I have expanded the scope of my research to include popular culture, but I am still intrigued by the mechanics of cultural policy and the challenges of studying the dynamics of major cultural phenomena. In broad terms, my research analyses Korea's cultural industries and cultural policy from the early twentieth century to the present.

Career highlights

Program Director, Master's in Contemporary Asian Studies (University of Amsterdam, 2005); Branch Head, International Institute of Asian Studies (University of Leiden, 2005); Program Coordinator, SEPHIS, International Institute of Social History (Amsterdam, 2004-05).
