URSULA HALL

Ursula Hall is a small and supportive community of young undergraduate students drawn from Australia and Overseas.

The Laurus Wing provides studios and one bedroom units with individual kitchens and bathrooms for postgraduate and later year undergraduate students. To view the constrution progress, click here.

Ursula Hall is managed by the ANU in an environment focused on academic and personal development. The Hall motto, ‘The truth shall set you free’, challenges each student to open themselves to the experiences of a wider world of knowledge and others.

Ursula Hall provides 206 rooms, each with a single bed, desk, chair, cupboard, pinboard, desk lamp, and washbasin. Students share single-sex bathrooms and toilets. Floorplan for a standard room

Ursula Hall's tariffs are listed in the Residential Fees summary.  Contact Ursula Hall for Guest or Group rates.

21 meals a week are provided, including a late dinner and packed lunch services. A vegetarian option is offered at every meal.

Your rent includes electricity, heating and water charges.

All rooms have phones with Voicemail. Internal university and Hall calls are free. Local, STD, and International calls are charged at competitive rates negotiated by the University.

For students wishing to bring their own computers, each room has a data-port linking directly to the University IT network. We also have two computer labs, with PCs, Macs and printers. Room internet usage is included in the weekly tariff.

It is an easy walk to shops, Union Court, lecture halls, Civic, and the National Botanical Gardens. Buses depart from outside the Hall to all major Town Centres in Canberra. There is secure storage for bicycles.

The Hall provides a strong network of pastoral and academic support. Each floor has its own Senior Resident dedicated to the pastoral support of 25 residents. A team of ten Academic Assistants provides effective and experienced help with the transition from high school to university. Two Sub-Deans and the Head of Hall lead the pastoral and academic support teams.

Ursula Hall has many talented music students and four rooms are available for music practice.

Ursula Hall has laundry and dryer rooms, these are available to all students and are free of charge. Residents provide their own laundry detergent.

A general carpark is located at the rear of the Hall. A multi-storey carpark with designated bays is also available for an extra fee. A University parking permit must be purchased to park on campus. Canberra's network of bike paths and public transport is quite good, so we encourage residents to save money and not bring a car.

Students are responsible for cleaning their own rooms. Vacuum cleaners can be borrowed from the Senior Resident. Recycling is strongly encouraged.

Smoking is prohibited in all University buildings. Designated smoking areas are located outside.

There are a small number of rooms available for students with physical disabilities. Please contact the Hall to discuss your needs.

Ours is an informal community of young people committed to study and learning. Our small size is a great advantage and lends a close-knit, family feel to the Hall. Students are encouraged, but not pushed, into participating in a wide variety of sports and arts activities. Our residents leave with lifelong friendships and a rich and varied student experience at the Australian National University.

Dr Ian Walker

Head, Ursula Hall
