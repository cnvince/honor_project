Professor John Love

MA (Camb), MMaths (Camb), MA (Oxf), DPhil (Oxf), DSc (Oxf)
Researcher in Guided Wave Photonics in the Physics Education Centre
ANU College of Physical and Mathematical Sciences
E: john.love@anu.edu.au
Flag this profile
Jump to: Biography|Researcher's projects|Student projects|Publications

Research interests

Theory and applications of guided wave photonics, especially in optical fibres, planar waveguides and light processing devices. 

Biography

Educated in applied mathematics at Cambridge and Oxford Universities, then spent 4 years as a postdoctoral researcher, first in the Department of Physics at the University of California at San Diego in the USA and then in the Department of Physics at the University of Toronto in Canada. Came to the Institute of Advanced Studies at the Australian National University in 1973 and worked as a Research Fellow on the theory of optical waveguides.

In 1989 established the first undergraduate half-course in fibre optics at the Australian National University that has since expanded into two full courses spanning optical fibre communications systems and the applications of fibre optics in wide range of other disciplines.

Researcher's projects

The publication of a book covering the development of fibre optics in Australia since 1965 from the perspective of the researchers involved in academia and industry.

Current student projects

Developing and simulating devices for the separate excitation and detection of each mode in few-mode fibres to help enhance the capacity of long-distance optical fibre communication systems.

Investigating fibre sensor methods for application over distances of the order of 100's of metres.
