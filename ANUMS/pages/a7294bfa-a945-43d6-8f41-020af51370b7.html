Digital Economy and Society Research Group

The World Wide Web (WWW) has changed how humans interact; for some scholars, we now live in the 'Age of the Network'. As online populations grow and the quantity and quality of interactions improve with new technology, social scientists are developing new approaches for using digital trace data to provide new insights into social, economic and political behaviour. The vastness and dynamic nature of the WWW poses research challenges relating to Big Data (storage, management and computation), which the emerging field of computational social science can address. The ADSRI Digital Economy and Society Research Group is conducting pioneering empirical social science research into online networks and developing new methods and tools to facilitate this research.
