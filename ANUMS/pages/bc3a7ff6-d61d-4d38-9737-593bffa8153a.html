Research interests

Identities, Attitudes, and Social Action.

I have a number of interests under this banner ranging across a number of social, religious and political attitudes and identities.  This research is mainly questionnaire-based. Projects within this area of interest include:

  1. The role of ideological variables like RWA and Fundamentalism in predicting a variety of social attitudes.  We make some important conceptual and statistical points about problems with RWA in this context and support a multidiemnsional interpretation of RWA.
  2. The importance of identities based on shared beliefs or opinions, and how those identities can be galvanised around shared emotions and norms for social (collective) action.
  3. The higher-order structure of social attitudes and how ideologies form around meaningful clusters of attitudes.
  4. How context and political frames can shape the salience and normative meaning of identities, attitudes and behaviour.

The categorisation process at all levels of social perception.

We are exploring the implications of the fundamental process of categorisation in social perception.  We apply this to impression formation, face processing, social memory, and judgements of homogenity and complexity. This research is largely experimental with implicit measures including free-recall, cued-recall memory, and reaction time as well as explicit impression and judgement measures.

Self, Identity and Normative processes in well-being/resilience, and approaches to learning in small and large groups.
