Biography

Stefan Keller completed his PhD. at the ANU in 2001, he then worked at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, California where he was a member of the MACHO team searching for the gravitational lensing signature of dark matter. He was also a member of the Djehuty team which constructed the first 3-d radiative hydrodynamical model of a stellar interior. In 2003, he returned to the ANU's Research School of Astronomy and Astrophysics for the SkyMapper project... and also for the beautiful southern sky.

Available student projects

Now is a perfect time to get involved with SkyMapper.
We have a broad range of potential SkyMapper-based PhD topics, ranging from the solar system to the edge of the optically observable universe!
I am proposing PhD programs in
  • Extremely Metal-poor Stars,
  • Galactic structure and chemical evolution, and,
  • The Magellanic Clouds
  • Hyper-velocity stars: these are stars ejected by close interaction with the central black hole of the Milky Way. They can be used to probe the distribution of dark matter in our galaxy.
